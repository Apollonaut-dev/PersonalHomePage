About Me


I'm Anthony and I created this website to show people who I am and what I can do. I also use it as a coding lab for my self-education in web development. You can see the backend code on GitHub.


I used this project to teach myself the basics of HTML, CSS and JavaScript DOM manipulation. I also created the backend in Node and so far it has been a great way to think about architecture. My next step is to connect a database and generate pages to serve requests dynamically. After that I'll learn the frameworks Express.js and Angular.js as well as Bootstrap HTML now that I have learned the basics of back and front end. Lastly (for now), I'll create a backend that I can easily publish articles and update my resume from. However, I will keep this website vanilla Node.js, vanilla HTML5, vanilla JavaScript and SCSS, so that I can experiment independently of the many frameworks that are out there and investigate the architecture of web development on my terms and at my own pace.


I used to go to the University of Waterloo but I failed out of the engineering program after struggling with my mental health. I saw a clinician and began reading; about mental health and about some of the topics that worried me. It wasn't that I was stressed out about school, it was that I was stressed out about everything else. That was a terrible part of my life but I learned much about myself in overcoming it. I'm back now and better than I've ever been. I'm looking for a software role to dedicate myself to as I complete my studies part-time.


This is a sample of my current level of competence. I have been working on it and the backend Node.js system for a few months part-time. I update it once a week, but I hope to do this more frequently as I put more work into the backend. I wanted to start with the frontend because I had only ever done backend work at my internships.


As for me personally? I have a passion for reading about philosophy which might be why I sometimes get lost in my own head. Currently, I am reading a book called On Computing by Paul Rosenbloom. I also enjoy listening to western classical music and exploring music from non-western cultures. I have recently developed a habit of going for early-morning runs and I have noticed that it makes me sharper during the rest of the day and that my mood, appetite, and productivity are more consistent. To de-stress and relax, I play video games such as Civilization and CS:GO, although the latter is not known for being particularly relaxing!


I've gone through a rough couple of years but I am back, immeasurably more mature, and ready to show the world of software what I am capable of.
